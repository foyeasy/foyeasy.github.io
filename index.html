Research Vision

My research vision centers on closing the gap between computational design and physical fabrication in advanced manufacturing. While investigating thermal degradation in metal cutting, I demonstrated that embedding conductive architectures into cutting inserts could reduce peak temperatures by over 50%. However, conventional machining restricts the fabrication of such topology-optimized, multi-material geometries. This limitation redirected my focus toward additive manufacturing (AM) as an enabling platform to materialize computationally derived designs with tailored thermal, mechanical, and functional properties. My ultimate research goal in the long run is to focus on interdisciplinary collaborations across design, modeling, and fabrication, specifically in the development of novel smart materials enabled by hybrid additive manufacturing processes.
